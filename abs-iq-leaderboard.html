How ABS IQ+ is built

ABS IQ+ is a four-component composite score scaled to 100 equals league average, following the wRC+ and ERA+ convention. Each component is z-score normalized within position group. One standard deviation above average equals 115.

Challenge rate is measured as challenges out of challengeable pitches: called balls or strikes where the player had challenges remaining and did not swing. The Statcast expected model incorporates pitch location, challenges remaining, runners on base, and the current count and out situation.

30%
Raw accuracy
Won% on challenges initiated. Did the pitch actually miss the zone?
20%
Skill accuracy
Won% above Statcast expected overturn rate. Winning on harder pitches signals genuine zone recognition.
30%
Discipline
Actual challenge rate vs. Statcast expected rate. Over-challenging is penalized. The emotional challenge is the primary ABS failure mode.
20%
Net value
Net challenges gained above expectation using the Statcast probability model.

Scale and interpretation

100 = league average. 115+ = elite. 107-114 = solid. 93-106 = average. Below 93 = losing value through poor challenge decisions.

Team IQ+ weights offense and defense equally. Within offense: accuracy 50%, discipline 50%. Within defense: accuracy 60%, volume 40%. Individual batter and catcher scores are computed within their own position groups.

Full methodology, component definitions, and scoring rationale are documented in the ABS IQ+ white paper. Expected model: Tom Tango and the Statcast team (tangotiger.com).
